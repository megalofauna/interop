Leading that answers to size

Body text sits at a comfortable leading past 1.5. The leading is a constant absolute space added over each element's own size, so it behaves the way legible type should at both ends of the scale.

As the type grows into headings the ratio tightens toward 1.3; as it shrinks toward captions and fine print it opens toward 1.8, keeping short lines from crowding. One clamped expression governs all of it.
